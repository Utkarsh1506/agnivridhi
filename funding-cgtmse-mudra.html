Collateral-Free Business Loans via CGTMSE & MUDRA

Access government-backed credit facilities without collateral requirements for micro and small enterprises.

What is CGTMSE?

Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE) is a scheme that provides credit guarantee to banks and financial institutions, enabling them to extend collateral-free credit to MSMEs. The guarantee cover is up to 85% of the sanctioned amount.

What is MUDRA?

Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana (PMMY) provides loans to micro/small business units through three categories:

Shishu

Up to ₹50,000
Startups & early stage businesses

Kishor

₹50,000 - ₹5 lakh
Established businesses needing expansion

Tarun

₹5 lakh - ₹10 lakh
Growing businesses requiring capital

Eligibility Criteria

  • CGTMSE: Micro and small enterprises as per MSMED Act with project cost up to ₹2 crore
  • MUDRA: Non-corporate, non-farm small/micro enterprises for income generating activities
  • Existing businesses and new ventures both eligible
  • Manufacturing, trading, and service sector enterprises
  • No collateral or third-party guarantee required for loans up to ₹10 lakh under CGTMSE
  • Borrower should have Udyam/MSME registration (recommended but not mandatory for MUDRA)

Key Benefits

  • No Collateral: Loans up to ₹10 lakh without any collateral or third-party guarantee
  • Lower Interest Rates: Competitive rates compared to unsecured business loans
  • Quick Processing: Faster approvals due to credit guarantee backing
  • Flexible Tenure: Repayment periods ranging from 3-7 years depending on loan type
  • Wide Coverage: Available through all scheduled commercial banks and RRBs
  • Business Growth: Working capital and term loans for expansion and operations

Loan Types Covered

  • Working capital for inventory, raw materials, and operational expenses
  • Term loans for machinery, equipment purchase, and business setup
  • Composite loans (working capital + term loan)
  • Loans for service sector activities
  • Transport vehicle loans for commercial use

Application Process

  1. Initial Assessment: Determine loan requirement and category (CGTMSE/MUDRA)
  2. Documentation: Prepare KYC, business proof, financials, and project details
  3. Bank Selection: Choose from empanelled banks/NBFCs offering best terms
  4. Loan Application: Submit application with supporting documents
  5. Appraisal: Bank evaluates business viability and credit history
  6. Sanction & Disbursement: Loan sanctioned and disbursed after approval

Required Documents

  • Identity Proof: Aadhaar, PAN Card, Voter ID
  • Address Proof: Utility bills, rent agreement
  • Business Proof: Registration certificate, GST, Udyam certificate
  • Financial Documents: Bank statements (6-12 months), ITR, balance sheet
  • Project Report: Business plan with cost estimates and revenue projections
  • Quotations: For machinery/equipment purchase
  • Photographs: Passport size photos of applicant
