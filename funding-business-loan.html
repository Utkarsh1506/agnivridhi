Lender Options We Connect You With

1. Public Sector Banks (PSBs)

  • Best for: Large loan amounts, government-backed schemes
  • Interest rates: 8.5% - 11.5% p.a.
  • Pros: Lower rates, CGTMSE/PMEGP schemes, higher loan amounts
  • Cons: Longer processing time, stringent documentation
  • Examples: SBI, PNB, Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank

2. Private Banks

  • Best for: Quick processing, digital experience
  • Interest rates: 9% - 14% p.a.
  • Pros: Faster approval, flexible terms, better service
  • Cons: Higher rates, lower loan-to-value
  • Examples: HDFC, ICICI, Axis, Kotak Mahindra

3. NBFCs (Non-Banking Financial Companies)

  • Best for: Fast disbursal, flexible criteria
  • Interest rates: 12% - 24% p.a.
  • Pros: Quick approval (24-72 hours), minimal documentation, easier eligibility
  • Cons: Higher interest rates, lower amounts
  • Examples: Bajaj Finserv, Tata Capital, Fullerton India, Lendingkart

Eligibility Criteria (General)

  • Business Age: Minimum 1-3 years of operations
  • Annual Turnover: ₹10L+ (varies by lender and loan type)
  • Credit Score: 650+ (PSBs), 600+ (private/NBFCs)
  • Profitability: Positive cash flow preferred
  • Collateral: Required for loans above ₹10L (or CGTMSE coverage)
  • Business Registration: Valid GST, Udyam/MSME certificate, PAN

Key Loan Features to Compare

  • Interest Rate: Fixed vs floating; compare effective cost
  • Processing Fees: 0.5% - 3% of loan amount
  • Prepayment Charges: 2-4% penalty or nil after certain period
  • Loan Tenure: 1-7 years (working capital), 3-20 years (term loans)
  • Moratorium Period: 3-12 months interest-only repayment
  • Collateral Requirements: Secured vs unsecured options

6. Disbursal & Post-Loan Support

  • Complete final documentation and legal formalities
  • Ensure timely fund disbursal to your account
  • Guide on optimal fund utilization and compliance
  • Set up EMI payment reminders and tracking

Common Loan Application Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying to too many lenders simultaneously (hurts credit score)
  • Incomplete or inconsistent financial documentation
  • Overstating turnover or profitability (leads to rejection)
  • Not checking credit score before applying
  • Choosing wrong loan product (e.g., term loan for working capital)
  • Ignoring fine print on prepayment, foreclosure, and penal charges
